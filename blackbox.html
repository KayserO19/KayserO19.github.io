Black Box Investigation

In my 9th grade media and technology class we were challenged to investigate "The Mystery Black Box". The mystery of the box is when water is poured through a funnel a different amount of water comes out each time for example we poured 1000ml of water and 1600ml came out, another example of this is when we poured in 1000ml and only 160ml came out. For this challenge we use many different platforms such as Adobe Photoshop and Autodesk Maya to design what we think is inside the Black Box and how it functions.

My Theory

My theory of The Mystery Black Box is that when water is poured in through a funnel there is a tube with 3 different holes. The 2 dividing tubes lead to 2 medium sized cups that when filled up to a specific amount the water will escape through the holes from the cup. When water enters through the middle tube they lead straight to the exiting cup. The speed that the water is poured in plays a big role on which tube the water leaves through for example if you pour it quickly more water will go through the middle tube, if poured more slowly more water will leave through the 2 diving tubes. When the 2 medium sized cups get filled to an amount water that was already stored it will stack and lots of water will come out making the amount of water that came out from the black box different than what you poured in.

Materials

  1. Cardboard
  2. Plastic
  3. Plastic Bucket
  4. Funnel
  5. Lots of Tape
  6. Hot Glue Gun

Procedure

We used the cardboard to make the box, the plastic was for the tubes and the medium sized cups, the plastic bucket was for the water that's leaving the box, the funnel is to guide the water getting poured in, tape was for support on tubes and cups, hot glue was for even more support. In the end when we put it to test and these were our results.
